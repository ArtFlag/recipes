Sourdough tortillas#

Yields 32 tortillas.

  • 125 g sourdough starter (discard or active, 100% hydration) (60)
  • 320 g warm water about 40c (160)
  • 56 g vegetable oil (25)
  • 2 teaspoons kosher salt (1)
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder (.25)
  • 510+ g all-purpose flour (250+)
  • Plus extra flour for dusting

  1. Mix sourdough starter, water, oil, salt, baking powder, and 500 g of flour in the bowl of a stand mixer and knead with the dough hook for 4-6 minutes or by hand, until dough comes together in a smooth, non-sticky ball. If itâ€™s too sticky, start adding the remaining cup of flour until you obtain the necessary consistency.

  2. Place dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a towel or plastic wrap and let it rest at room temperature for about 30 minutes.

  3. Divide the dough into about 32 (16) equal pieces. 30 grams per piece.

  4. Roll each piece of dough into a ball. Use the traction of the counter to shape the balls.

  5. Cover dough pieces with a towel and let them rest for 5 minutes. Start rolling each dough piece into a circle that measures out about 17 cm in diameter.

  6. Start cooking the tortillas by heating up a cast iron frying pan or a non-stick frying pan over medium high heat.

  7. When the pan is hot, add a tortilla. Cook on one side for about 1 minute, flip it over and cook on the other side for about 30 seconds. Cook it until itâ€™s just slightly browned.

  8. Lay out cooked tortillas on a baking sheet and let them cool slightly before placing them in a tortilla warmer or closed container.
